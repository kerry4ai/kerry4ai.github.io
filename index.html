About

I am an Associate Professor at the China Institute for Studies in Energy Policy, School of Management, Xiamen University. My research focuses on applied econometrics, energy economics, and environmental economics.

I am particularly interested in understanding the dynamics of energy efficiency, carbon productivity, and the impact of environmental regulations on technological innovation. My work employs advanced econometric methods, including spatial stochastic frontier models and convergence analysis.

I have published extensively in leading journals such as Energy Economics, Energy Policy, Technological Forecasting and Social Change, Applied Energy, and The Stata Journal.

Research Interests

Applied Econometrics

Developing and applying econometric methods for policy analysis, including spatial econometrics, stochastic frontier analysis, and convergence models.

Energy Economics

Studying energy efficiency, energy productivity, and the economics of renewable energy technologies. Analyzing the rebound effect and energy demand dynamics.

Environmental Economics

Investigating environmental regulations, green technology innovation, carbon emissions, and the pathways to sustainable development.

Industrial Economics

Examining industrial structure upgrading, environmental regulation impacts on industry, and the relationship between FDI and pollution.
